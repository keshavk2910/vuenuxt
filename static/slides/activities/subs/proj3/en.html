Canaan Urban Greening

It was in the aftermath of the January 2010 earthquake when people began building in the vast area known as Canaan, and they created their city without any support from the Haitian government or NGOs. Rapid urbanization in an environmentally vulnerable area comes with many challenges, but communities carved out spaces for playing fields and public parks, a delineated a web of roads.

In partnership with local community leadership known as Neighborhood Committees, Acceso social enterprise, and American Red Cross, J/P HRO piloted three innovative strategies:

  1. Urban agroforestry: Support to individuals in Canaan to start small tree nurseries and to plant in no-build zones as well as other vulnerable areas. Combining livelihood enhancement with environmental protection
  2. Green and Gray Infrastructure: In one of the largest ravines in Canaan, a combination of traditional ravine correction tactics combined with green infrastructure including trees and grasses have had a visible impact in preventing flooding
  3. Mini-Forests: In this method, a very dense patch of trees, shrubs and grasses/cacti are planted together to mimic a forest. The strategy creates an ecological system where one plant supports another, and where ground cover helps to retain moisture, while an outer ring of tough grasses and cacti prevent goats and other animals from eating the trees

The strategies in Canaan have been pulled together to form the basis of an urban greening toolkit. Read more on our blog here
